Special Places

Castle of Dachau

Dachau Castle on the Schlossberg hill in Dachau was for a long time the preferred summer residence of the Wittelsbach dynasty. In the 16th century, the medieval castle was turned into a Renaissance palace, which was partially remodeled in the Baroque period, and only one of its four wings has survived to this day.

Concentration Camp

Dachau - the meaning of this name cannot be erased from German history. It stands for all concentration camps that National Socialists established in their area of rule.

Church of St. James

The Catholic parish church of St. James in Dachau in the Upper Bavarian district of the same name is a late Renaissance building erected towards the end of the 16th and beginning of the 17th century on the site of a Romanesque predecessor church. The church, at the crossroads of three long-distance roads, to Augsburg, to Freising and to Munich, is dedicated to the Apostle James the Elder, the patron saint of pilgrims and travelers.
